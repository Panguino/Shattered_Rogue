## 🏃 Run Structure Comparison (3D Action Roguelite Focus)

This section focuses specifically on the **run-based loop** that defines action roguelites — the structure Shattered Rogue will follow.

### Run Format Comparison (Core Reference Games)

| Game                  | Run Length | Run Structure                                         | Win Condition                | Post-Win Option                  | Difficulty Scaling               |
| --------------------- | ---------- | ----------------------------------------------------- | ---------------------------- | -------------------------------- | -------------------------------- |
| **Risk of Rain 2**    | 30–90 min  | Stage → Teleporter → Boss → Next Stage (5 stages)     | Escape via Moon/Commencement | Loop (restart harder)            | Timer-based (constant)           |
| **Crab Champions**    | 20–40 min  | Island → Clear waves → Choose upgrade → Next island   | Beat final boss island       | Harder difficulty modes          | Per-island scaling               |
| **Megabonk**          | 15–30 min  | Arena → Survive waves → Level up                      | Survive as long as possible  | Endless (score-chasing)          | Time-based enemy scaling         |
| **Gunfire Reborn**    | 40–60 min  | Room → Clear → Shop/Scroll → Boss → Next Act (3 acts) | Beat final boss              | Reincarnation levels (8)         | Per-Reincarnation mods           |
| **Returnal**          | 1–3 hrs    | Room → Clear → Choose path → Boss → Next biome (6)    | Beat Act 1 & Act 2           | Score chasing, trait unlocking   | Fixed per biome, scaling enemies |
| **Hades**             | 20–40 min  | Chamber → Clear → Choose boon → Boss (4 biomes)       | Escape the Underworld        | Heat system (Pact of Punishment) | Heat modifiers (voluntary)       |
| **Vampire Survivors** | 30 min     | Survive 30 minutes → Reaper arrives                   | Survive 30 min / kill Reaper | —                                | Time-based wave escalation       |
| **Nuclear Throne**    | 10–20 min  | Area → Clear → Advance (7 worlds)                     | Reach Nuclear Throne         | Loop system (infinite)           | Per-loop escalation              |
| **Everspace**         | 1–2 hrs    | Sector → Zones → Jump → Boss sector (7 sectors)       | Reach final sector           | —                                | Per-sector scaling               |

### The Ideal Run Structure for Shattered Rogue

Based on the most successful 3D action roguelites:

- **Target run length:** 20–40 minutes (sweet spot for "one more run")
- **Structure:** Arena/Stage → Clear enemies → Choose upgrades → Boss → Advance
- **Win condition:** Defeat final boss after 4–6 stages
- **Post-win:** Loop system with escalating difficulty (Risk of Rain 2 / Nuclear Throne model)
- **Endless mode:** Optional score-chasing mode for leaderboards

### Achievement Design Patterns (What Works Best)

1. **Per-Character Completion Marks** (Binding of Isaac, Gunfire Reborn, Risk of Rain 2)
   - Beat each difficulty with each character = trackable grid of completions
   - Creates natural goal-setting and character mastery incentive
   - **Best for Shattered Rogue:** ✅ Each squid variant × each difficulty = completion matrix

2. **Quest-Based Unlocks** (Megabonk, Crab Champions)
   - "Kill 50 elites with the Shotgun" → Unlock new weapon
   - Specific objectives tied to content unlocks
   - **Best for Shattered Rogue:** ✅ Quest chains unlock new squid abilities/weapons

3. **Discovery/Collection Log** (Risk of Rain 2, Binding of Isaac, Balatro)
   - Find every item/enemy/boss → completionist satisfaction
   - Logbook/Bestiary/Codex tracks discovered content
   - **Best for Shattered Rogue:** ✅ Space creature codex + item encyclopedia

4. **Difficulty Milestone Achievements** (Dead Cells Boss Cells, Gunfire Reborn Reincarnation)
   - Beat higher difficulties → unlock harder modes + bragging rights
   - **Best for Shattered Rogue:** ✅ Escalating difficulty tiers with unique rewards

5. **Seeded Daily/Weekly Challenges** (Nuclear Throne, Returnal, Spelunky 2)
   - Same run seed for all players → compete on leaderboards
   - **Best for Shattered Rogue:** ✅ Daily challenge with seeded RNG

## 🎯 What Players Love Most (Cross-Game Patterns)

Based on community surveys, Steam reviews, Reddit discussions, and player sentiment analysis:

### 1. **Build Variety & Synergies** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The #1 driver of replayability. Players return to discover new item/ability combinations.

- **Best Examples:** Binding of Isaac (700+ items), Risk of Rain 2 (stackable items), Noita (infinite wand combos), Balatro (150+ Jokers)
- **Key Insight:** The moment a player discovers a "broken combo" is peak engagement

### 2. **"One More Run" Loop** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

Short, self-contained runs that feel meaningful even in failure.

- **Best Examples:** Vampire Survivors (30-min cap), Hades (death = story), Slay the Spire (45-min runs)
- **Key Insight:** Runs should be completable in 20–45 minutes for optimal engagement

### 3. **Satisfying Power Scaling** ⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐

The "zero to hero" arc within a single run — starting weak, ending godlike.

- **Best Examples:** Risk of Rain 2 (exponential stacking), Vampire Survivors (screen-filling power), Crab Champions (wild synergies)
- **Key Insight:** The power fantasy must escalate dramatically — players want to feel broken

### 4. **Meaningful Choices & Risk/Reward** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Every decision should feel impactful — choosing items, routes, difficulty.

- **Best Examples:** Slay the Spire (route pathing), FTL (resource tension), Hades (God choice at doors)
- **Key Insight:** Players should face genuine dilemmas, not obvious "best choices"

### 5. **Tight, Responsive Controls** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Combat must feel precise and rewarding on a moment-to-moment basis.

- **Best Examples:** Dead Cells (fluid animation canceling), Hades (responsive dashing), Returnal (DualSense feedback)
- **Key Insight:** "Game feel" is non-negotiable — juicy VFX, screen shake, responsive input

### 6. **Narrative Integration** ⭐⭐⭐⭐

Story woven into the gameplay loop, not tacked on.

- **Best Examples:** Hades (death = story beats, rarely repeated dialogue), Returnal (psychological horror through cycles), Inscryption (meta-narrative)
- **Key Insight:** Death should advance story, not just reset it

### 7. **Meta-Progression (Sense of Overall Progress)** ⭐⭐⭐

Unlocking new content between runs to keep things fresh.

- **Best Examples:** Rogue Legacy (castle upgrades), Hades (Mirror of Night), Dead Cells (cell unlocks)
- **Key Insight:** Balance is critical — too much meta-progression trivializes skill

### 8. **Accessibility Options** ⭐⭐⭐

Making the game approachable to newcomers without sacrificing depth.

- **Best Examples:** Hades (God Mode — gradual damage resistance), Celeste-style assist modes
- **Key Insight:** Accessible ≠ easy. Let players dial difficulty to their level

## 📈 2024–2025 Genre Trends

Based on ongoing market research:

1. **Hybrid Genres Dominate** — Pure roguelikes are niche; roguelite elements fused with action/FPS/strategy dominate sales
2. **Co-op is Growing** — Gunfire Reborn, Crab Champions, Sworn (2025) — multiplayer roguelikes are an underserved market
3. **Bullet Heaven / Survivors** — VS-like games (Brotato, DRG: Survivor, Megabonk) continue exploding in popularity
4. **3D Roguelikes Expanding** — Risk of Rain 2 proved the formula; more 3D entries coming
5. **Narrative Emphasis** — Post-Hades, players expect some form of story integration
6. **Accessibility Modes** — God Mode / difficulty sliders becoming standard
7. **"No-Grind" Philosophy** — Some devs (Crab Champions) are pushing back against mandatory meta-progression
8. **AI-Driven Content** — Procedural generation becoming more sophisticated; community desires varied early-game experiences

### 🎯 Why Players Say These Games Succeeded (Community Analysis)

Based on Reddit threads, Steam review analysis, and player interviews:

#### 1. **"The Hook is Instant"** — Immediate Gratification

> _"I was hooked within 5 minutes of my first run."_ — Common sentiment for Vampire Survivors, Balatro, Hades

- Players know if they like a roguelike within ONE run
- Low barrier to first fun moment is critical
- **Lesson for Shattered Rogue:** First run must feel amazing even if you die in 2 minutes

#### 2. **"I Discovered a Broken Combo"** — Emergent Discovery

> _"The moment I stacked 15 glasses and became invincible, I was hooked for 200 hours."_ — Risk of Rain 2 player

- Players share "I found THIS" moments on Reddit/Twitter/YouTube
- Clip-worthy moments drive organic marketing
- **Lesson:** Design stackable systems with emergent "broken" combos intentionally

#### 3. **"Just One More Run"** — Session Length Sweet Spot

> _"I told myself I'd play one more run. That was 4 hours ago."_ — Slay the Spire player

- 15–40 minute runs hit the sweet spot
- Too short = no investment, too long = commitment anxiety
- **Lesson:** Target 20–30 min runs with natural stopping points

#### 4. **"Dying Doesn't Feel Bad"** — Death Reframing

> _"In Hades, I actually want to die sometimes because of the story."_ — Hades player
> _"In Isaac, I die and immediately think 'next time I'll...' instead of ragequitting."_

- Death must feel like a learning opportunity, not punishment
- Narrative death (Hades) or meta-unlock death (Dead Cells) or pure "I know what I did wrong" (Spelunky)
- **Lesson:** Make death satisfying — post-run stats, story beats, unlock progress

#### 5. **"The Price was Right"** — Value Perception

> _"$5 for Vampire Survivors gave me 300 hours. Best money I've ever spent."_

- Low price + massive replayability = incredible perceived value
- Many top roguelikes are $10–25, not $60
- **Lesson:** Price aggressively ($15–25 range), let value speak through playtime

#### 6. **"Streamers Played It"** — Viral Discovery

> _"I bought Balatro because I saw Northernlion play it."_

- Roguelikes are naturally stream-friendly (short runs, high variance, emotional moments)
- Streamer discovery is the #1 marketing channel for indie roguelikes
- **Lesson:** Design for clip-worthy moments. Ensure spectator-friendly UI

#### 7. **"Every Run Feels Different"** — Variety as Content

> _"500 hours in and I'm still finding item combos I've never seen."_ — Isaac player

- Procedural generation is cost-effective content for small teams
- Deep item/ability pools create exponential variety
- **Lesson:** Invest heavily in item/ability count and synergy design

### 📊 Genre Economics Summary

| Metric                             | Average (Indie Roguelikes)                | Best-in-Class                         |
| ---------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------- |
| **Team size at launch**            | 2–10 people                               | 1 (Balatro, VS, Crab Champions)       |

> **Bottom Line for Shattered Rogue:** A small indie team (2–5 people) with a $15–20 price point, Early Access launch, streamer-friendly design, and 20–30 minute runs is the proven formula. The 3D co-op niche (Risk of Rain 2, Crab Champions, Gunfire Reborn) is underserved and commercially validated — this is exactly where Shattered Rogue should position.
