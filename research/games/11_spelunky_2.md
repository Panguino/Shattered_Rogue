# Spelunky 2 (2020) — Mossmouth (Derek Yu)

> **Genre:** Platformer Roguelike | **Perspective:** 2D Side-scrolling | **Players:** 1–4 Local + Online | **Steam Rating:** 92% Positive

## 🎮 Core Concept

Explore procedurally generated caves, collect treasure, avoid traps, and survive. Pure skill-based roguelike — no meta-progression. Every pixel is dangerous, and the game's systemic interactions create emergent chaos.

## 🧩 Systemic Interactions (Core Innovation)

The magic of Spelunky is in its systems interacting:

- Drop a torch → Oil on ground ignites → Fire spreads → Destroys shop → Shopkeeper attacks
- Whip an arrow trap → Arrow fires → Hits enemy → Enemy drops item → Item triggers another trap
- Use bomb near water → Water flows → Drowns enemies → Fills lava
- Throw a dead body at a mine → Mine explodes → Creates new path

## 📈 Progression — Pure Skill

### In-Run

- Bombs, ropes, items from shops/crates
- Gold for shop purchases
- Companion NPCs (hire from coffins/shops)
- Mount animals (turkeys, axolotls)

### Meta-Progression: **NONE**

- No permanent upgrades
- No unlockable power
- Shortcuts can be built (skip worlds) but with worse loadout
- Knowledge and skill ARE the progression
- This is the original roguelike philosophy

## 🌟 Key Innovations

1. **Systemic Emergent Gameplay** — Every element interacts; chaos emerges from simple rules
2. **No Meta-Progression** — Pure skill mastery; knowledge is power
3. **"Authored Randomness"** — Templates + rules create levels that feel designed despite being procedural
4. **shopkeeper AI** — Aggressive NPC that hunts you across floors if angered
5. **Cosmic Ocean** — Near-infinite looping endgame (7-1 to 7-99)
6. **Community Mysteries** — Hidden paths and secrets took months for the community to discover
